Part 1 — Congruence (13A)

Question 1 · 13A Q1a
Are these two triangles congruent? Give a reason.
Show answer
Triangle ABC has a tick on AB and BC, and a dot on angle C.
Triangle DEF has a tick on EF and DE, and a dot on angle F.

AB = DE (one tick), BC = EF (one tick), and angle C = angle F (dot).
Two sides and the included angle are equal → SAS congruence.
Yes, △ABC ≅ △DEF (SAS).
Question 2 · 13A Q1c
Are these two triangles congruent? Give a reason.
Show answer
Triangle ABC: tick on AB, dot on angle B, cross on BC.
Triangle DEF: tick on DE, dot on angle D (but placed at D, not matching), cross on EF — wait, checking marks: dot on B and dot on D is at a different vertex relative to the ticked sides.

AB = DE (tick), BC = EF (cross), but the equal angle (dot) is at B (between the two marked sides in △ABC) and at D (which is not between the two marked sides DE and EF in △DEF).

The equal angle is not between the two equal sides in both triangles → SSA, which does not guarantee congruence.
Cannot be determined as congruent — SSA is not a valid congruence condition.
Question 3 · 13A Q1e
Are these two triangles congruent? Give a reason. (The figure shows triangles XYZ and DEF sharing a common rectangle outline, with right angles at Y and F, and equal tick marks on two sides.)
Show answer
Both triangles have a right angle (90°).
XZ = DF (hypotenuse, double tick) and XY = DE (single tick).

Right angle + hypotenuse + one other side equal → RHS congruence.
Yes, △XYZ ≅ △DEF (RHS).
Question 4 · 13A Q1f
Are these two triangles congruent? Give a reason.
Show answer
Triangle ABC: angles α at B and β at C, tick mark on BC.
Triangle DEF: angles α at D and β at E, tick mark on DF.

Angle B = angle D (both α), angle C = angle E (both β), and BC = DF (tick).
Two angles and the side between them are equal → AAS congruence.
Yes, △ABC ≅ △DEF (AAS).
Question 5 · 13A Q2a
Four triangles are shown below. Two of them are congruent. Which pair is it, and why?
Show answer
All four triangles have sides involving 5 m and 7 m and angle α.

Triangle A: sides 5 m, 7 m with α opposite the 7 m side (not between the marked sides).
Triangle B: sides 5 m, 7 m with α between the two sides → SAS setup.
Triangle C: sides 7 m, 5 m with α opposite the 7 m side — same as A.
Triangle D: sides 7 m, 5 m with α between them — same as B.

A and C share the same two sides and angle in the same configuration.
B and D share the same two sides and the included angle.

Triangles A and C are congruent (SAS — same two sides and included angle arrangement).
Also, triangles B and D are congruent (SAS).

Part 2 — Similarity (13C)

Question 6 · 13C Q1a
Show that the figure below contains similar triangles. (Triangle PQS and triangle PTR share a right angle at Q and S respectively.)
Show answer
In the figure, PQS and the larger triangle PTR:
• Angle P is common to both triangles.
• Angle PQS = angle PST = 90° (both right angles, shown by the square markers).

Two angles equal → AA similarity.
△PQS ∼ △PTR (AA).
Question 7 · 13C Q1b
Show that the figure contains similar triangles. (Two triangles ABC and DEC overlap at point C, with 40° angles marked at B and at C on one triangle, and 40° at C on the other.)
Show answer
In triangles ABC and EDC:
• Angle ACB = angle DCE (vertically opposite angles).
• Angle ABC = angle DEC = 40° (marked equal).

Two pairs of equal angles → AA similarity.
△ABC ∼ △EDC (AA).
Question 8 · 13C Q1f
Show that the figure contains similar triangles. (Triangle ABC contains point D on AB and point on BC, with tick marks indicating AD = DC and DB = BC — actually DB = DC, forming an inner triangle BDC.)
Show answer
In triangles ABC and DBC:
• AD = DC (single tick) and DC = DB (single tick), so AD = DB = DC — D is equidistant, meaning △ADC is isosceles.
• Angle ABС = angle DBC (same angle at B, shared).
• Since AD = DC, triangle ADC is isosceles → angle DAC = angle DCA.
• Angle BDC = angle DAC + angle DCA = 2 × angle BAC (exterior angle of △ADC).
• This gives angle BDC = 2 × angle BAC, and combined with the shared angle at B:

Angle ACB = angle DBC and angle ABC is common → AA similarity.
△ABC ∼ △DBC (AA).

Question 10 · 13C Q5
A 1.8 m tall person stands 4 m from a lamppost and casts a shadow 3 m long.
Find the height of the lamppost.
Show answer
The person and the lamppost both cast rays from the same light source to the tip of the shadow.
This creates two similar triangles (both share the angle at the shadow tip).

Small triangle: height 1.8 m, base 3 m (shadow length).
Large triangle: height h, base 3 + 4 = 7 m (shadow + distance to post).

Ratio of corresponding sides: h / 1.8 = 7 / 3
h = 1.8 × 7/3 = 1.8 × 2.333… = 4.2 m

The lamppost is 4.2 m tall.
