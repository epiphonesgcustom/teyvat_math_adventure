Review Set 8A — Measures (p. 162)

Question 1 · 8A Q2
The diameter of a car tyre is 50 cm. How many revolutions does the tyre complete if the car travels 2 km?
Show answer
Circumference = πd = π × 50 cm = 50π cm
Distance = 2 km = 200 000 cm

Revolutions = 200 000 / (50π) = 4000 / π ≈ 1273 revolutions
Question 2 · 8A Q4
Find, in terms of π, the perimeter and area of the shaded region below. The square has side 2 m, and the four petal shapes are each a semicircle of diameter 1 m (radius ½ m) fitting inside the square.
Show answer
Each petal is made from two quarter-circle arcs of radius 1 m (the full side).
There are 4 petals, each bounded by two arcs. Each arc is a quarter of a circle with radius 1 m.

Perimeter: 8 quarter-circle arcs of radius 1 m
= 8 × (¼ × 2π × 1) = 8 × π/2 = 4π m

Area of square: 2² = 4 m²
Area of 4 full circles of radius 1 m = 4 × π(1)² = 4π m²
But the 4 circles overlap at the centre. By inclusion-exclusion, total circle area inside square = 4 × (half-circle area) = 4 × π/2 = 2π.
Shaded area = 2π − (area of square − 4 × semicircle) ...
Simpler approach: each petal = 2 × (quarter-circle − triangle) = 2 × (π/4 − ½) = π/2 − 1
4 petals = 4(π/2 − 1) = 2π − 4 m²
Question 3 · 8A Q5a
Find the surface area of the cuboid with dimensions 8.4 cm × 5.1 cm × 3.6 cm.
Show answer
A cuboid has 3 pairs of rectangular faces.
SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)
= 2(8.4×5.1 + 8.4×3.6 + 5.1×3.6)
= 2(42.84 + 30.24 + 18.36)
= 2 × 91.44
SA = 182.88 cm²
Question 4 · 8A Q5b
Find the surface area of the open-top cylinder with radius 9.7 mm and height 38.3 mm.
Show answer
Open-top cylinder: 1 base circle + curved surface (no top).
Radius = 19.4/2 = 9.7 mm

Base area = πr² = π × 9.7² = 94.09π
Curved SA = 2πrh = 2π × 9.7 × 38.3 = 743.02π

Total SA = 94.09π + 743.02π = 837.11π
SA ≈ 2629 mm²

Review Set 13B — Congruence & Similarity (p. 272)

Question 6 · 13B Q1
Three triangles are shown. State which pair is congruent, giving a reason.
Show answer
Triangle A: sides 5 m and unknown, angle α — no right angle.
Triangle B: sides 5 m, angle α, right angle marker — RHS candidate.
Triangle C: sides 5 m, angle α, right angle marker — same as B.

Triangles B and C both have: a right angle, the same side 5 m, and angle α.
Two angles and a corresponding side are equal → AAS.
Triangles B and C are congruent (AAS).
Question 7 · 13B Q2
In parallelogram ABCD, [DX] is the angle bisector of angle ADC, and [BY] is the angle bisector of angle ABC. Prove that triangles DXY and BYX are congruent.
Show answer
In a parallelogram: AB ∥ DC and AB = DC, AD ∥ BC and AD = BC.
Also, opposite angles are equal: angle ADC = angle ABC.

Since DX bisects angle ADC: angle XDC = ½ angle ADC
Since BY bisects angle ABC: angle YBC = ½ angle ABC
∴ angle XDC = angle YBC (both = half of equal angles)

Also: DC = AB (opposite sides of parallelogram), so DY ...
In triangles DXY and BYX:
• XY is common to both triangles.
• angle DYX = angle BXY (alternate angles, DX ∥ BY since both bisect equal alternate angles in the parallelogram)
• angle DXY = angle BYX (same reason)

Two angles and the included side (XY) are equal → ASA congruence.
△DXY ≅ △BYX (ASA).
Question 8 · 13B Q5a
Show that the triangles UVZ and XZY are similar, then find x.
Show answer
In the figure, angle θ is marked at both U (in △UVZ) and at X (in △XZY).
Also, angle UZV = angle XZY (vertically opposite angles)... actually angle Z is shared or vertically opposite.
Looking at the diagram: the two triangles share angle Z (the crossing point), and both have angle θ marked.

Two pairs of equal angles → AA similarity: △UVZ ∼ △XZY.

Corresponding sides are proportional:
UV/XZ = VZ/ZY = UZ/XY
10/8 = 5/x
x = 5 × 8/10 = 5 × 0.8
x = 4 cm
Question 9 · 13B Q5b
Show that the triangles ABC and DEC are similar, then find x.
Show answer
BC is a transversal crossing AB ∥ DE (indicated by the arrows).
angle ABC = angle DEC (alternate angles, AB ∥ DE)
angle BAC = angle EDC (alternate angles)

Two pairs of equal angles → AA similarity: △ABC ∼ △DEC.

Corresponding sides:
AC/DC = BC/EC = AB/DE
x/5 = 3/8 → x = 15/8
x = 1.875 cm ≈ 1.88 cm
Question 10 · 13B Q5c
Show that triangles JKM and JML are similar, then find x.
Show answer
In the figure, angle J is common to both triangles JKM and JML.
The right angle is at K (in △JKM) ... checking: from the image, M is on JL, and KM appears to be an altitude. So angle JMK = 90°? Actually looking at the diagram: it appears JK and ML are sides, with M on JL and K below.

More carefully: angle J is shared, and angle JKM = angle JML = 90° (both right angles).
Two angles equal → AA similarity: △JKM ∼ △JML.

Corresponding sides:
JK/JM = JM/JL
x/(3x−2) = (3x−2)/(2x−3+3x−2) — using the side labels from the diagram:
JK = x, KM = (3x−2), ML = (2x−3)

By similar triangles: JK/JM = KM/ML
x/(3x−2) = (3x−2)/(2x−3)
x(2x−3) = (3x−2)²
2x² − 3x = 9x² − 12x + 4
0 = 7x² − 9x + 4 ... this doesn't factor cleanly, so let's use the ratio differently:
JM² = JK × JL (geometric mean relation)
(3x−2)² = x × (x + 3x−2) = x(4x−2)
9x²−12x+4 = 4x²−2x
5x²−10x+4 = 0
x = (10 ± √(100−80))/10 = (10 ± √20)/10
x = (10 + 2√5)/10 ≈ 1.45 m (taking the positive solution that gives positive side lengths)
