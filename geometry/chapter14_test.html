Question 3 · 14A Q3
A circle has diameter AB = 11 cm. The chord AC = 7 cm. Find the length of chord BC.
Show answer
Since AB is a diameter, angle ACB = 90° (angle in a semicircle).
Triangle ABC is right-angled at C.
BC² = AB² − AC² = 11² − 7² = 121 − 49 = 72
BC = √72 = 6√2
BC ≈ 8.49 cm

Part 3 — Radius & Tangent (14C)

Question 6 · 14C Q1a
O is the centre. Find x, giving reasons. (Two radii form an isosceles triangle with angle 30° at base.)
Show answer
Two radii from O to points on the chord form an isosceles triangle.
The base angle is 30°. Since the triangle is isosceles, both base angles equal 30°.
Angles in a triangle sum to 180°
x + 30 + 30 = 180
x = 120°
But x is the angle at the circumference on the other side — actually x° is the angle at the top vertex of the isosceles triangle formed by two radii.
x = 180 − 30 − 30 = 120°

(Alternatively: the central angle = 180° − 2×30° = 120°. The inscribed angle at the circumference subtending the same arc = 120°/2 = 60° — but x here is at the centre, so x = 120°.)
Question 7 · 14C Q1c
Find a and b, giving reasons. Angles 40° and 30° are given, with a tangent at the base.
Show answer
The radius OT meets the tangent at T at a right angle (tangent ⊥ radius).
∴ b = 90°

The two radii OA and OT are equal, so triangle OAT is isosceles. The base angles are equal, meaning the angle at T inside the triangle (between OT and chord AT) also equals 40°.

The full angle at T between the tangent and the radius is 90°, so the angle between the chord AT and the tangent:
a = 90° − 40° = 50°

a = 50°, b = 90°

Part 5 — Tangent–Chord Angle (14E)

Question 10 · 14E Q1a
C is the point of contact of tangent CT. Angle BCA = 40°, angle BAC = 60°. Find x (angle BCT).
Show answer
By the tangent–chord angle theorem (alternate segment theorem):
The angle between a tangent and a chord equals the inscribed angle in the alternate segment.

Angle ACT = angle ABC (alternate segment) = 180° − 60° − 40° = 80°
Since BCT is a straight line at the tangent: angle BCA + angle ACT = angle BCT + angle BCA ...
Actually: angles BCA = 40°, angle BAC = 60°, so angle ABC = 80°.
x = angle BCT = angle BAC (alternate segment) = 60°...

Wait — from the diagram: 40° is angle BCA (between chord BC and chord CA), and x° is angle BCT (between chord BC and tangent CT).
By alternate segment theorem: x = angle BAC = 60°... but alternate segment is the angle in the far segment.
Angle ABC = 80°. Alternate segment theorem: angle ACT = angle ABC = 80°.
Tangent is straight: angle BCT = 180° − angle BCA − angle ACT = 180° − 40° − 80° = 60°... Hmm.
Directly: x = angle BCT = alternate segment angle = angle BAC = 60°

Part 6 — Same Segment & Cyclic Quadrilateral (14F)

Question 11 · 14F Q1a
Find x, giving a reason. (128° is an inscribed angle; x° is another inscribed angle subtending the same arc.)
Show answer
x° and 128° are opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral (all four vertices lie on the circle).
Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180°.
x + 128 = 180
x = 52°

Part 7 — Mixed Circle Angles (14G)

Question 12 · 14G Q1a
Find x, giving a reason. (Intersecting chords inside the circle, with 46° marked.)
Show answer
Two chords cross inside the circle, creating two pairs of vertically opposite angles at the point of intersection.
x° and 46° are vertically opposite angles.
Vertically opposite angles are equal.
x = 46°

(The other pair of angles at the intersection each measure 180° − 46° = 134°.)
