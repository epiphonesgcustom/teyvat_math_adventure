Review Set 8B — Measures (p. 162)

Question 1 · 8B Q1a
Find the perimeter and area of the isosceles triangle with base 8 cm, equal sides 5 cm, and height 5 cm (dashed).
Show answer
Perimeter: 5 + 5 + 8 = 18 cm

Area: = ½ × base × height
The height bisects the base, so h² = 5² − 4² = 25 − 16 = 9 → h = 3 cm.
Area = ½ × 8 × 3 = 12 cm²
Question 2 · 8B Q1b
Find the perimeter and area of the trapezium with parallel sides 6 m and 3 m, height 5 m, and one right angle.
Show answer
Area: = ½(a + b) × h = ½(6 + 3) × 5 = ½ × 45 = 22.5 m²

Perimeter: The slanted side = √(5² + (6−3)²) = √(25 + 9) = √34 ≈ 5.83 m
P = 6 + 5 + 3 + 5.83 ≈ 19.83 m
Question 3 · 8B Q1c
Find the perimeter and area of the half-annulus (semicircular ring) with outer diameter 10 cm and width 2 cm.
Show answer
Outer radius R = 5 cm, inner radius r = 5 − 2 = 3 cm.

Area: = ½π(R² − r²) = ½π(25 − 9) = 8π ≈ 25.13 cm²

Perimeter: outer semicircle + inner semicircle + 2 straight edges
= πR + πr + 2(R − r)
= 5π + 3π + 2(2)
= 8π + 4 ≈ 29.13 cm
Question 4 · 8B Q2
A rhombus has diagonals of length 12 cm and 20 cm. Find its perimeter.
Show answer
The diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at right angles.
Half-diagonals: 6 cm and 10 cm.
Side = √(6² + 10²) = √(36 + 100) = √136 = 2√34
Perimeter = 4 × 2√34 = 8√34 ≈ 46.6 cm
Question 5 · 8B Q3a
Write a formula for the area A of the pentagon shape (rectangle + triangle on top) with base b, rectangle side c, triangle top a, and height h.
Show answer
The shape is a rectangle of width b and height c, plus a triangle of base b and height h sitting on top.

Area of rectangle = b × c
Area of triangle = ½ × b × h

A = bc + ½bh

Review Set 13A — Congruence & Similarity (p. 272)

Question 6 · 13A Q1a
Determine whether the two triangles ABC and DEF are congruent, giving reasons. (Right angles at B and E, equal tick marks on AB and DE, and equal tick marks on BC and EF.)
Show answer
Triangle ABC: right angle at B, AB marked equal to DE, BC marked equal to EF.
Triangle DEF: right angle at E, same side marks.

Two sides and the included right angle are equal.
RHS (Right angle, Hypotenuse, Side) — but here we have two legs equal, not hypotenuse.
With angle B = angle E = 90°, AB = DE, BC = EF → SAS congruence.
△ABC ≅ △DEF (SAS)
Question 7 · 13A Q1b
Determine whether triangles PQR and XYZ are congruent, giving reasons. (Angles α and β marked on both, with equal sides.)
Show answer
Both triangles have angles α and β marked, with the side between them equal (tick marked).
Two angles and the included side are equal → ASA congruence.
△PQR ≅ △XYZ (ASA)
Question 8 · 13A Q2
In quadrilateral ABCD, AB ∥ DC and AB = DC (shown by double tick marks). Show that △ABC ≅ △CDA, and hence deduce ABCD is a parallelogram.
Show answer
In △ABC and △CDA:
• AB = DC (given, equal sides)
• AC is common to both triangles
• angle BAC = angle DCA (alternate angles, AB ∥ DC)

Two sides and an included angle... actually: AB = DC, AC common, and angle BAC = angle DCA (alternate angles).
This is SAS: △ABC ≅ △CDA (SAS)

Therefore BC = DA (corresponding sides of congruent triangles), and angle ABC = angle CDA.
Since AB ∥ DC and BC = DA, ABCD has both pairs of opposite sides equal → ABCD is a parallelogram.
Question 9 · 13A Q5a
Show that triangles UVW and XVY possess similar triangles (V is the intersection point, angle UVX = angle WVY vertically opposite, and 24° marked at both U and X).
Show answer
In triangles UVW and XVY:
• angle YUV = angle YXV = 24° (both marked)
... wait, from diagram: 24° is at Y (top-right) and 24° is at X (bottom-left near W).
• angle UVW = angle XVY (vertically opposite angles at V)
• Therefore the third angles are also equal.

Two pairs of equal angles → AA similarity.
△UVW ∼ △XVY (AA)

Question 12 · 14A Q1c
Find a. (Cyclic quadrilateral with angles 41°, 102°, and a°.)
Show answer
From the diagram: the angles 41°, 102°, and a° are all inscribed angles in the circle, forming a cyclic quadrilateral.
The angle a° and 102° are in the same triangle inside the circle. Looking more carefully:
Two chords create a triangle. The 41° and a° are angles in the same segment or related by the cyclic quadrilateral rule.

In the cyclic quadrilateral: opposite angles sum to 180°.
If 102° and a° are opposite: a = 180 − 102 = 78°.
But 41° is also present. The angles of a triangle on the circle: 41° + 102° + third = 180° → third = 37°.
Inscribed angles subtending the same arc: a = 37° ... or by the exterior angle theorem for cyclic quads.
Exterior angle of cyclic quadrilateral = opposite interior angle:
a = 41° + 102° − 180°? No.
Most directly: a = 180° − 41° − 102° = 37° (angles in the triangle formed by the three chords).
Question 13 · 14A Q3a
Find x. (Two chords from the same point on the circle: angle (x + 14)° and x° are inscribed angles subtending the same chord.)
Show answer
From the diagram, x° and (x + 14)° are angles in the same segment (both inscribed angles subtending the same arc from the same side) — but they are different sizes, so they subtend different arcs.

Looking again: they appear to be opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral:
x + (x + 14) = 180
2x + 14 = 180
2x = 166
x = 83°
Question 14 · 14A Q3c
Find x. (Tangent–chord angle: x° inside the circle, 38° between chord and tangent.)
Show answer
The 38° is the angle between the tangent and a chord.
By the alternate segment theorem: the tangent–chord angle equals the inscribed angle in the alternate segment.
x = 38° ... but x and 38° appear to be supplementary in the diagram (same segment).

From the diagram: x° is an inscribed angle and 38° is the tangent–chord angle on the same side.
By alternate segment theorem: x = 38°... or if x is the angle in the same segment:
x + 38 = 180° → x = 142°...

Most directly from the diagram (inscribed angle opposite the tangent-chord angle):
x = 180° − 2 × 38° = 104°...
The isosceles triangle formed (two equal chords, tick marks): base angles both = 38° (alternate segment), so apex = 180 − 38 − 38 = x = 104°.

Question 21 · 16B Q3
Triangle ABC has angle BAC = θ, AB = 12 cm, AC = 14 cm, and area = 33.4 cm². Find the two possible values of θ.
Show answer
Area = ½ × AB × AC × sin θ
33.4 = ½ × 12 × 14 × sin θ
33.4 = 84 sin θ
sin θ = 33.4 / 84 = 0.3976…

Or the obtuse angle: 180° − 23.44° = 156.56°

θ ≈ 23.4° or θ ≈ 156.6°
Question 22 · 16B Q4
Find all unknown sides and angles of the triangle with angles 43°, 38°, base 23.8 m, and unknowns x m and y m.
Show answer
Third angle θ = 180° − 43° − 38° = 99°

By sine rule: 23.8 / sin 99° = x / sin 43° = y / sin 38°

Find x (opposite 43°):
x = 23.8 × sin 43° / sin 99°
x = 23.8 × 0.6820 / 0.9877 ≈ 16.44 m

Find y (opposite 38°):
y = 23.8 × sin 38° / sin 99°
y = 23.8 × 0.6157 / 0.9877 ≈ 14.83 m
